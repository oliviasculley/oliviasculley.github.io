Portfolio

20 August 2020 in portfolio

This page is a centralized listing of my professional work and will be updated as time goes on. In addition, you can find my resume here.

Junlab

(Apr 2019 – May 2020)

At the Jun Laboratory, I had the opportunity to build a “virtual twin” of the lab’s current robotic setup in VR. Along the way, I had the opportunity to expand the virtual twin into a more ambitious project, NERVV: an open-source, easily-integratable C# framework to visualize and control material-production pipelines in virtual reality with Unity 3D and ROS. In addition, I was also able to create a toolkit of novel, VR-based interaction techniques that can be applied to any machine implementing NERVV’s framework.

Purdue Virtual Reality Club

(Apr 2019 – May 2020)

The Purdue Virtual Reality Club is an active club of virtual reality enthusiasts at Purdue University. Meeting weekly, activities include keeping up with the latest industry news and experiences and trying the latest VR/AR devices thanks to the Purdue Envision Center. While the only requirement for membership is a passion for immersive technologies, the club also focuses on VR/AR development in Unity 3D, which I worked to promote. As an officer, I hosted learning sessions in technologies such as A-frame and Unity 3D, mentored members in VR development, and lead educational club-wide projects through the PVRC’s Github page. Finally, as the treasurer, I helped to manage overall finances and plan events. The PVRC can be found on Facebook, Discord and Github.

Envision Center

(Jan 2019 – Aug 2020)

The Purdue Envision Center is part of Purdue’s Research Computing division, and provides virtual simulation, multimedia production and data visualization services to faculty and industry clients alike. While at the Envision Center, I was able to work on a number of projects with some amazing coworkers, which I’ve listed in [this post here]({% post_url 2019-08-08-envision %}). Most of these projects are open-source and able to be run in a browser, so I’d highly encourage you to check them out!

CS 390-VR, CS 390-VRT

(Aug 2018 – Dec 2019)

I had the opportunity to help lead course development for CS 390-VR and CS 390-VRT for Dr. Gustavo Rodriguez-Rivera at Purdue University. This course served as an introduction to virtual reality and real-time graphics development with game engines such as Unity 3D and Unreal Engine. As an undergraduate teaching assistant, I developed and delivered bi-weekly lectures, created and graded assignments, and led lab sessions where students worked with VR headsets such as the HTC Vive and Oculus Rift.

Chopralab

(Feb 2018 – Aug 2018)

At the Purdue Chopralab, I mainly worked on the mobile version of MINT, which visualizes and manipulates protein ligands. As a result, I second-authored a research publication which is available on ChemRxiv. I also helped design a minimalist and responsive lab website with a news feature using Jekyll, and created the MINT website linked above.

Rival Esports

(Feb 2016 – Sep 2017)

While I was in high school, I helped create motion graphics for Rival Esports, a Rocket League and Battlerite Esports tournament. Giving away hundreds of dollars and streaming to thousands of live viewers during our weekly Rocket League tournaments, we even managed to get featured on the frontpage of twitch.tv! I utilized my previous experience in compositing and motion graphics to produce animated lower thirds, transitions and graphics that worked with OBS. Combined with custom video-game modding technology, PRL was quickly known for visual excellence that easily surpassed any other broadcasters in the area and emulated the quality of professional sports broadcasts.

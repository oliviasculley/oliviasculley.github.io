# Junlab

*(Apr 2019 -- May 2020)*

At the [Jun Laboratory](https://web.ics.purdue.edu/~jun25/index.html), I had the
opportunity to build a "virtual twin" of the lab's current robotic setup in VR.
Along the way, I had the opportunity to expand the virtual twin into a more
ambitious project, [NERVV](https://gitlab.com/oliviasculley/nervv): an open-source,
easily-integratable C# framework to visualize and control material-production
pipelines in virtual reality with Unity 3D and ROS. In addition, I was also able
to create a toolkit of novel, VR-based interaction techniques that can be
applied to any machine implementing NERVV’s framework.

# Purdue Virtual Reality Club

*(Apr 2019 -- May 2020)*

The Purdue Virtual Reality Club is an active club of virtual reality enthusiasts
at Purdue University. Meeting weekly, activities include keeping up with the
latest industry news and experiences and trying the latest VR/AR devices thanks
to the Purdue Envision Center. While the only requirement for membership is a
passion for immersive technologies, the club also focuses on VR/AR development
in Unity 3D, which I worked to promote. As an officer, I hosted learning
sessions in technologies such as [A-frame](https://aframe.io/) and [Unity
3D](https://unity.com/), mentored members in VR development, and lead
educational club-wide projects through the PVRC's
[Github](https://github.com/pvrc) page. Finally, as the treasurer, I helped to
manage overall finances and plan events. The PVRC can be found on
[Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/groups/777681062280083/),
[Discord](https://discord.gg/2bnDcKE) and [Github](https://github.com/pvrc).

# Envision Center

*(Jan 2019 -- Aug 2020)*

The Purdue Envision Center is part of [Purdue's](https://www.purdue.edu/)
[Research Computing division](https://www.rcac.purdue.edu/), and provides
virtual simulation, multimedia production and data visualization services to
faculty and industry clients alike. While at the Envision Center, I was able to
work on a number of projects with some amazing coworkers, which I've listed in
[this post here]({% post_url 2019-08-08-envision %}). Most of these projects are
open-source and able to be run in a browser, so I'd highly encourage you to
check them out!

# CS 390-VR, CS 390-VRT

*(Aug 2018 -- Dec 2019)*

I had the opportunity to help lead course development for [CS
390-VR](https://www.cs.purdue.edu/homes/cs390vr/) and CS 390-VRT for [Dr.
Gustavo Rodriguez-Rivera](https://www.cs.purdue.edu/people/faculty/grr) at
Purdue University. This course served as an introduction to virtual reality and
real-time graphics development with game engines such as Unity 3D and Unreal
Engine. As an undergraduate teaching assistant, I developed and delivered
bi-weekly lectures, created and graded assignments, and led lab sessions where
students worked with VR headsets such as the HTC Vive and Oculus Rift.

# Chopralab

*(Feb 2018 -- Aug 2018)*

At the [Purdue Chopralab](http://www.chopralab.com/), I mainly worked on the
mobile version of [MINT](https://chopralab.github.io/MINT/index.html), which
visualizes and manipulates protein ligands. As a result, I second-authored a
research publication which is [available on
ChemRxiv](https://chemrxiv.org/s/de3acf1e4d7f20b0bd7e). I also helped design a
minimalist and responsive lab website with a news feature using Jekyll, and
created the MINT website linked above.

# Rival Esports

*(Feb 2016 -- Sep 2017)*

While I was in high school, I helped create motion graphics for [Rival
Esports](https://rivalesports.com/), a Rocket League and Battlerite Esports
tournament. Giving away hundreds of dollars and streaming to thousands of live
viewers during our weekly Rocket League tournaments, we even managed to get
featured on the frontpage of [twitch.tv](https://www.twitch.tv/rivalesports)! I
utilized my previous experience in compositing and motion graphics to produce
animated lower thirds, transitions and graphics that worked with OBS. Combined
with custom video-game modding technology, PRL was quickly known for visual
excellence that easily surpassed any other broadcasters in the area and emulated
the quality of professional sports broadcasts.
